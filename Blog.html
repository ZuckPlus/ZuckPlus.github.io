My Web Development Journey

Week 1: Setting Up My Blog

This week, I created my blog page using HTML and hosted it on GitHub Pages. This is the start in my road to becoming a better web developer. I spent time structuring my blog to ensure it is easy to navigate and visually appealing. The Blog might change as I learn new tools and design The goal of this blog is to document my progress and share insights as I learn new web development tools and technologies.

To start, I have chosen two tools to focus on: Tailwind CSS and Node.js. Over the next two weeks, I will dive deeper into these technologies, experimenting with their features and applying them in practical projects. These tools will enhance my ability to create efficient, modern, and scalable web applications.

Additionally, I have developed a rubric to evaluate each new tool I learn. This rubric will help me assess ease of learning, real-world usefulness, performance, customization, community support, scalability, and industry adoption. Using this structured approach, I aim to make informed decisions about which technologies to integrate into my workflow.

Rubric for Evaluating Web Development Tools

Category Criteria 1 - Poor 2 - Fair 3 - Good 4 - Excellent
Ease of Learning How easy is it to learn and get started? Steep learning curve, hard to understand Requires effort, but manageable Clear documentation, beginner-friendly Intuitive, well-documented, easy adoption
Practical Use How useful is it for real-world web projects? Limited real-world use Niche use cases Commonly used in development Industry standard, widely adopted
Performance How well does it perform in terms of speed and efficiency? Slow, bloated, or inefficient Some performance concerns Generally fast and optimized High performance, lightweight
Customization & Flexibility How much control does it give developers? Rigid, difficult to customize Some flexibility, but limited options Good level of customization Highly flexible, adapts to many needs
Community & Ecosystem How strong is its community and support? Small or inactive community Limited support/resources Active community, decent support Large community, lots of plugins/tools
Scalability Can it handle large projects and scale well? Struggles with larger applications Works for medium projects Scales well with optimizations Highly scalable and used in enterprise apps
Adoption & Longevity Is it widely used, and will it stay relevant? Rarely used or outdated Somewhat popular but declining Actively used with ongoing support High industry adoption, future-proof
